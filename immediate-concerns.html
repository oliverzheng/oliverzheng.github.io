I'm always amazed at the confidence and certainty my young kids have in their answers to nearly all questions about their preferences. When asked which option they want, they very quickly choose and rarely waver in their choice.

I imagine I was that way too as a young child. Though over time, I've learned more sophisticated ways of thinking and decision making that I can run my consciousness through a process to come up with answers for harder questions. While these processes give more thoughtful answers, they are also slower and sometimes create additional emotional side effects.

"What if this answer isn't good enough?"
"Is there a backup in case this is wrong?"
"Does this align with my long term vision of X?"
"Do I really have to give up Y for this?"

I suppose these are actually intended as inputs for learning how to better the decision making process in the future. But it sure feels a lot less simple than when I could just pick without having to think twice.
